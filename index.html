Abstract

Recent advances in dataset distillation aim to reduce dependence on extensive datasets by synthesizing compact, representative datasets. Nevertheless, current methods struggle with cross-task generalizability, limiting their practical applicability.

As a solution, we propose SIN, an embarrassingly simple yet effective and novel dataset distillation paradigm whereby images are directly zipped into thumbnails and stitched together to form a larger synthetic image. Specifically, we establish various experts in SIN, including the Pruning Expert for data pre-pruning, the Zipping Expert for zipping and stitching thumbnails, and the Augmentation Expert for enhancement. In addition, we incorporate an evaluation phase into this paradigm --- in which two pathways are introduced to better utilize synthetic data, releasing the potential of the synthetic data.

The experiment results of classification task and object detection task demonstrate that our SIN not only achieves competitive performance, but also has strong cross-task adaptability. The project will be presented at this website: https://fofilix.github.io/.
